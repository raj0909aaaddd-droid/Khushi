Dear Khushi,

There are some people who enter your life and the very first thing you notice is that something feels different — lighter, warmer, easier. The air shifts a little. You are not entirely sure how to explain it, but you know it almost immediately. That is how I felt when you came into my life. And today, I just want to say — out loud, in words, on paper — how genuinely glad I am that you are here.

Welcome, Khushi. Not just into a friendship, but into something that I already know is going to matter. Because people like you — the kind who carry warmth in the way they speak, who make others feel seen without even trying — do not come around very often. And when they do, you hold on. You pay attention. You make sure they know they are valued.

I want you to know that this place — this friendship, this space between us — is yours entirely. There is no performance required here, no version of yourself you need to carefully present. You can be honest, you can be messy, you can be uncertain, and you will still be more than welcome. That is not something I say lightly. It is something I mean in every sense of the word.

New friendships have a particular kind of magic to them — the excitement of discovering someone, the quiet thrill of realising you understand each other in ways you did not expect, the strange comfort of someone who is new but somehow already feels familiar. That is what I feel with you, Khushi. Already familiar. Already easy. Already something I am grateful for.

I hope you know that you bring something to every room you enter — a kind of brightness that is yours alone. The way you engage with the world, the curiosity you carry, the genuine warmth you offer to people — these are not small things. They are rare things. And I see them. I see them clearly, and I want you to know that they are appreciated more than you might realise.

I am looking forward to all of it — the long conversations that stretch past midnight, the comfortable silences that do not need to be filled, the shared laughter over things that probably would not make sense to anyone else. I am looking forward to the moments we have not had yet, and the memories we have not made yet. All of it. Every small, ordinary, extraordinary bit of it.

So here is my welcome to you — not with ceremony or formality, but with complete and utter sincerity. This friendship is a good thing. You are a good thing in my life. And I do not take that lightly, not even for a single moment. You belong here, Khushi. Fully, genuinely, without any condition or reservation — you belong here.

Here is to the beginning of something really, truly beautiful. I could not be more glad that it is with you.

✦
With the warmest of welcomes,
and a friendship already cherished —
Your friend
